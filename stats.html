Statistics


Here are the facts when it comes to cable service in the United States.
  • Gizmodo reports that "When a competing cable company is present, your cable bill typically goes down by 15-percent, and service generally improves" (Gizmodo).
  • Gizmodo also says that "a mere 2-percent of American markets had a choice of cable providers" (Gizmodo).
  • The White House says that "three out of four Americans do not have a choice between providers for Internet at 25 Mbps, the speed increasingly recognized as a baseline to get the full benefits of Internet access" (WhiteHouse.gov).
These statistics show that lack of competition in cable service is a major problem for a large number of Americans. By introducing competition, however, service can be improved. See how you can make a difference →
